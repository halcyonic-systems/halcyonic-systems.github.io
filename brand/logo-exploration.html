Three visual concepts for a brand identity grounded in the Common Core Theorem: seven independent definitions of “system,” one shared categorical structure.

K ≅ 2 — a system is a morphism f : R → T

Concept 01

The H as Morphism

Recommended: Logo / Mark

The letter H is the walking arrow. Two verticals (domain, codomain) connected by a directed crossbar (the morphism). Works at every scale from favicon to print. Only those who know category theory see the arrow — everyone else sees a clean lettermark.

The strongest practical logo because it’s the initial of the brand name, encodes the core theorem literally, and the meaning is hidden in plain sight. The “H” in Halcyonic has always been the morphism — we just hadn’t drawn it yet.

Concept 02

The Walking Arrow

Recommended: Front Page Hero

One arrow drawing itself into existence. Click the source to see seven traditions converge. Click the target to see three frontiers emerge. The blue-to-gold gradient encodes the transformation from relations to things.

The strongest interactive experience. The draw animation gives it narrative arc — you watch the morphism come into existence, then explore what it connects. It teaches the Common Core Theorem without explaining it. Visitors arrive at the insight through interaction, not exposition.

Concept 03

The Commuting Triangle

Recommended: About / Architecture Page

Three vertices — Labs, Systems, Ventures — connected by directed edges that draw themselves in sequence. Click all three to reveal the commutativity condition: the path from insight to impact is the same regardless of route.

The most organizationally specific concept. It encodes the three-layer architecture (Labs generates, Ventures applies, Systems holds) as a categorical diagram. A universal insight meets a particular org. Best for visitors who want to understand how Halcyonic works, not just what it studies.

Site Integration Notes

Shared DNA

Both systems use Inter for body text and Cormorant Garamond (already loaded on the Research page). The type system is shared — only the color tokens change.

Site Mapping

The Bridge

The Research page already diverges toward this aesthetic — Cormorant Garamond headings, warm gold accents, editorial card system. It’s the natural integration point between the current scholarly site and the atmospheric canvas language.

Carry Over Regardless

The interaction language — click-to-reveal, hover-to-learn, draw-on-load. The current site has almost no interactivity. Even in light mode, depth-on-interaction would make it feel alive.
